The Outline

For my final project, I plan to explore Chinese consumers' attitudes toward cultural and creative products. The cultural and creative industry has been rapidly emerging, known for its highly innovative works that span a wide range of styles and appeal to diverse audiences. Through data analysis, I aim to investigate consumer psychology, demographic groups, and the market share of different types of cultural and creative products.

The key data I intend to work with includes consumers’ purchase intentions and motivations regarding cultural and creative products, as well as the demographic distribution of consumers—specifically their age, occupation, and the range of how much they spend on cultural and creative products. Additionally, the preferred categories of products within this industry.

The Data

My data comes from MobTech and SeedHangyan, both of which are open-source platforms (access requires only a registered account). The information is primarily in the form of reports and textual explanations, so I will need to extract and organize the relevant data manually.

Method and Medium

For this project, I plan to collect and analyze data from open-source industry reports provided by MobTech and SeedHangyan. I will manually extract and organize the relevant information, focusing on consumer behavior and demographics. To visualize and communicate my findings, I will use Tableau to create interactive charts and graphs that highlight patterns in consumer motivations, preferences, and spending behavior. I may also use Shorthand to build a narrative-style presentation of my research outcomes.

In addition, I’m interested in experimenting with Flourish due to its wide range of customizable chart templates and creative features—such as icons, color adjustments, and interactive elements—which allow for more expressive storytelling. While Flourish offers flexibility and is easy for beginners, I have found that it may have limitations in terms of data depth and professional approaches compared to Tableau. Thus, it may be one of my starting points before I get things systemized in Tableau.
